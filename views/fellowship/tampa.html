Who We Are

Welcome to the Interprofessional Advanced Fellowship in Clinical Simulation (IAFCS) at the VA Tampa Healthcare System in Tampa, Florida. We provide cutting-edge simulation experiences that enhance health care safety and elevate educational opportunities. At the core of our mission lies a commitment to ensuring the safety of both patients and staff. Through rigorous training, we aim to strengthen the knowledge, skills, and attitudes of health care professionals, fostering stronger, more cohesive interprofessional teams. By doing so, we empower our fellows to deliver exceptional care to our Veterans and caregivers, ultimately enhancing health outcomes and overall well-being.

Overview of the Tampa VA Simulation Program


The Blaine A. Markee Simulation Center at the Tampa VA is where innovation meets excellence in health care education. The center stands at the forefront of simulation training, holding an advanced certification from the Simulation Learning, Evaluation, Assessment, and Research Network (SimLEARN) – a distinction shared by only a select few VA facilities nationwide. The Tampa VA has robust partnerships with the University of South Florida’s (USF) medicine, nursing, pharmacy, and physician assistant programs. With more than 225 full-time equivalents for physician residents, the facility is one of the most active VA sites supporting medical resident education. The Tampa VA owns a mobile simulation bus which has provided training not only locally but throughout the state of Florida. Each year, the simulation team and faculty help train more than 8,000 participants.

The simulation center actively conducts training in mock codes, code stroke, change in condition, the trauma nursing core course, code team leadership, nursing skills fairs, resident/fellow procedural boot camps, point-of-care ultrasound, and powered air purifying respirator/personal protective equipment. The Blaine A. Markee Simulation Center has several collaborations with community partners including but not limited to the Center for Advanced Medical Learning and Simulation (CAMLS), USF, the University of Tampa and other local nursing school programs. The co- directors are currently collaborating with the VISN 8 Patient Safety Center of Inquiry on an Assisted Falls Virtual Reality Pilot project and SimLEARN on an Activations Readiness Assessment as the Tampa VA prepares to open a new bed tower.

The Tampa VA is designated as a 1A VA center, reserved for VA facilities with the highest levels of volume, patient risk, teaching, and research. Additionally, the hospital has specialties in rehabilitation, spinal cord injury, mental health, and acute care.
