Core Curriculum & Program Domains

The IAFCS coordinating center directs and implements the overall strategy for developing core domains during the year-long fellowship experience by:

  • Overseeing the development of an overall foundational national curriculum with contributions from the local sites.
  • Ensuring both national and local curricula, including fellow assessments, align with the core domains.
  • Scheduling periodic check-ins with fellows to discuss activities at local fellowship sites.

    • Core Curriculum and Experiential Learning

    Check out the Core Curriculum Calendar

    According to the Society for Simulation An integral aspect of the fellowship will be experiential learning through active participation in medical center activities to develop and complete substantive simulation projects. We expect fellows will have the opportunity to serve on and support local facility and national committees to learn about various aspects of health care delivery with the perspective of how simulation can promote meeting project objectives. Through the development of the capstone project, fellows will be mentored and coached in leadership and change management skills.

    The core curriculum  consists of twice monthly virtual conferences – one specifically directed towards fundamental concepts in clinical simulation (DIDACTIC series) and the other highlighting recent developments and specialty areas (LEARN series). These conferences also provide fellows the opportunity to present their ongoing work with their capstone project. Part of the core curriculum are two SimLEARN courses: Introduction to Clinical Simulation (ICS) and Faculty Instructor Course (SFIC). ICS is a virtual course while SFIC is face-to-face. Funding is provided by SimLEARN for fellow’s travel to the national simulation center in Orlando, Florida.

    Fellows will work on National simulation projects building their professional network and being part of a national team working towards improving Veteran Care. Fellows will also have access  to virtually attend multiple communities of practice (COP) meetings, including SimLEARN Simulation COP and Resuscitation COP. In their final quarter, fellows will present their capstone project on the SimLEARN Community of Practice Call. Fellows will also have opportunities to submit articles to the SimLEARN Newsletter, a national publication.

    During the fellowship program, IAFCS fellows are expected to attend a fellowship-wide conference, held either at SimLEARN in Orlando, Florida, or in a virtual environment. Funding is also provided for each fellow to attend the annual International Meeting on Simulation in Healthcare (IMSH) hosted by the Society of Simulation in Healthcare (SSH).

    For further information about each local site, please contact the co-directors of each site.

    Testimonials

    Joseph Nadler, MD, Tampa, FL IAFCS Fellow 2021-2022

    Currently: Internal Medicine Staff Physician, VA Palo Alto Health Care


    “My year spent at the James A. Haley Veterans Hospital Advanced Interprofessional Fellowship in Clinical Simulation was incredibly beneficial for my career. I spent the year developing my skills in simulation and medical education where I was able to impact the way code blues are run and taught at our hospital from the organizational, physician, and nursing sides. During my project ‘Using Simulation to Improve the Use of Continuous Waveform Capnography’, I was able to get training in quality improvement and use that to integrate myself into different aspects of the VA organization that I would otherwise not be exposed to, such as, different committees, supply chain and logistics. This fellowship not only gave me dedicated training on how to be adept at simulation, but also gave me a holistic view of how VA hospitals are run. The skills and knowledge I gained during the fellowship helped me get hired into a dream job at the Palo Alto VA as an internal medicine hospitalist/nocturnist with extra non-clinical time to develop projects and teach using simulation.”
